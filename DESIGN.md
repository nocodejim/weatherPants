# WeatherPants Design System

## Look and Feel
The WeatherPants application utilizes a refreshing and airy design aesthetic, taking deep inspiration from clear skies, varying weather conditions, and natural atmospheric gradients. By heavily employing soft curves, significant elevation, and airy negative space, the interface achieves a modern "floating" or "cloud-like" feel.

## Color and Light
The color palette centers around calming meteorological tones—sky blues and cloud grays—grounded by deeper storm hues. Sunset orange provides a warm, energetic contrast for secondary actions or distinct elements. Backgrounds employ subtle gradients (either warm or cool) to simulate the changing times of day or varying weather conditions, creating an immersive atmosphere that feels more alive than flat colors. High contrast is maintained for text elements to ensure readability against both light card backgrounds and gradient canvases.

## Typography
Typography is kept clean and minimalist by leveraging a light sans-serif font family. The hierarchy is extremely pronounced: large, bold display text ensures the most critical information (like the current temperature) is instantly readable at a glance, while smaller, sturdy labels add necessary context without cluttering the visual field. 

## Layout and Spatial Design
The spatial design leans heavily into the "cloud-like" motif. Content is housed within generously padded, distinctly elevated cards featuring large corner radii (up to 24dp). This approach breaks the layout into easily digestible chunks that appear to float above the atmospheric background gradients. Significant vertical and horizontal spacing (up to 60dp for titles and 32dp for content padding) prevents the interface from feeling dense or overwhelming, giving the user's eyes plenty of room to rest. The visual weight is directed primarily toward the weather card, cascading down to the supplementary advice card.
